01
The challenge

Two very different tracking flows, one daily habit

Tracking strength training loads and tracking macros have nothing in common in how they're logged: one happens at the gym between sets, the other at the table or in the kitchen. Both needed to stay fast to use day to day, without either tracking flow being sacrificed for the other.

02
The approach

An AI scanner to remove input friction

Instead of making the user search for every food item in a database, a scanner automatically detects the foods present in a photo (several at once) and suggests an adjustable macro estimate before adding it to the journal. On the training side, direct import of an existing program (PDF, TXT, CSV) avoids manually re-entering every exercise.

Key features
01
Load tracking per session
Weight and reps logged set by set, with superset support and a reminder of the last performance on each exercise.
02
Program import
An existing plan in PDF, TXT, or CSV format is parsed and automatically split into sessions and exercises, ready to follow with no re-entry.
03
AI meal scanner
A single photo is enough to detect several foods at once and estimate their macros, with an adjustable portion before adding it to the journal.
04
Retail food database
Search branded products with their real nutritional values, alongside manual entry and the AI scanner.
05
Statistics and streaks
Trends over 7, 30, or 90 days, goals reached per macro, and two separate streaks for nutrition and training.
06
Community and leaderboards
User search, follows, and leaderboards by session, volume, or streaks, with programs that can be made public or private.
